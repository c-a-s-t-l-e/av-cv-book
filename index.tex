\markboth{Preface}{Preface}

This book's goal is to give you an overview of the world of
transportation.

This will hopefully give you the know-how to generally understand:

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  the bureaucratic structures,
\item
  domain-specific terminology, and
\item
  technological steps
\end{itemize}

requisite to becoming useful in this domain.

Just like lots of things in transportation, this book is definitely not
perfect and should always be considered a work in progress. Feel free to
reach out to me if you think something could be improved or would be a
good addition to the text.

As always though, press on!

\chapter{Bureaucracy}\label{bureaucracy}

Basic infrastructure (think interstates, highways, and common roads) is
considered a common good by most people. With that said, how it's built,
how it's maintained, and how people use it have generally fallen into
the domain of government.

As with most government institutions, there are also various layers of
bureaucracy.

This chapter will go through the layers that exist in the world of
transportation.
